estimate modal frequencies and damping (Q / decay) from the IR,

compute the room eigenmodes (analytic for a cuboid, numerical for shaped ceilings),

render pressure maps (top/side slices), and

search an optimal listening position that minimizes deep cancellations + resonant unevenness.

Two important realities up front:

From a single IR at one mic position you can’t uniquely reconstruct the full 3D pressure field. The IR gives you a “projection” of the room onto that source–receiver configuration.

But if you combine (a) the geometry model (eigenmodes) with (b) modal parameters extracted from the IR, you can produce a physically consistent field and a very useful seat optimizer.

Below is the full math + algorithm (ready to turn into code).
