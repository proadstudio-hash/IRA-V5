3) Peak table mixes “measured-delay” lengths with “predicted-geometry” distances (internal mismatch)

In the Peak Table you report:
	•	Delay (ms), dL (m), L_refl (m) (these appear consistent with measured delay: dL ≈ c*delay, and L_refl = L_direct + dL)  ￼
	•	but you also report P*, |S-P*|, |P*-M| which are distances derived from the predicted specular point (geometry).  ￼

These two can disagree (and they do). That creates confusion because a user expects:
L_{refl} \approx |S-P^*| + |P^*-M|
…but it won’t match if L_refl is computed from measured delay while P* is computed from predicted specular geometry.

✅ Fix (best practice): show both sets explicitly:
	•	L_refl_meas = L_direct + c*Delay_meas
	•	L_refl_pred = |S-P*| + |P*-M|
	•	dL_meas = c*Delay_meas
	•	dL_pred = c*PredDelay

Then the “Err (ms)” naturally ties to Delay_meas - PredDelay.

4) Schroeder frequency looks wrong for the reported room + RT

You report Schroeder Freq ≈ 291–297 Hz.  ￼
But from the report:
	•	Room volume V = 4.146 \cdot 2.55 \cdot 2.7 \approx 28.5\ \mathrm{m^3}  ￼
	•	RT60 ≈ 0.176 s (fused average)  ￼

Using the common estimate:
f_s \approx 2000\sqrt{\frac{RT60}{V}}
gives roughly 157 Hz, not ~291 Hz.

✅ Fix: re-check:
	•	constant used (2000 vs 4000),
	•	volume units (m³ vs something else),
	•	whether you accidentally used area or one dimension instead of volume,
	•	whether you used EDT/T30 differently (still wouldn’t double it this much).
